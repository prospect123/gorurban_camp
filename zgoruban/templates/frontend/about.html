ABOUT GORURBAN



Go Rurban a combination of Rural + Urban
youth movement aiming to create understanding and sensitivity about the highlights and shortcomings of both the rural and the urban lifestyles.

It is a collaboration between Ekta Parishad, ANSH Happiness Society, and other partner organizations. The name Go Rurban is a combination of the words “rural” and “urban”. This initiative, besides having led to several youth camps, is also a broader campaign where urban youth can explore and experience rural backgrounds, so the gap between the urban and rural dimensions can be bridged.

HISTORY GORURBAN



In February 2017, Rajagopal P.V, founder of Ekta Parishad along with a group of 7- 8 people from different organizations and field areas, started their walk from Sheopur, MGSA in a village named Ekta Nagar along with expressing their idea for peace and non-violence.

Rajagopal started his peace-building by working alongside a devoted Gandhian J.P. Narayan and Subba Rao to disarm 578 dacoits (outlaws) in the Chambal region of India in 1972. Thereafter the young Gandhian turned away from dealing with the direct violence and the cycle of revenge of dacoits, to the indirect violence suffered by Adivasis, bonded laborers, and other landless communities affected by poverty.

The walk was a village yatra covering diverse villages from Madhya Pradesh, accompanying different sessions and activities on Peace and Non Violence. Moreover, the yatra brought the idea of Urban Youth engagement towards the rural area and how youth can contribute to the increasing gap of Rural and Urban.

This yatra resulted in a youth initiative known as RURBAN - GO RURBAN which is a combination of RURAL + URBAN. It is not just a camp but a campaign to connect rural and urban youth. The basic idea of the camp is to build a stronger bridge to cover the gap between rural and urban society.


Through such initiatives, we can develop a better understanding of peace and non-violence in youth and can ensure their participation in struggles for land and other means of livelihood, irrespective of their urban or rural background simultaneously conducting Rurban internships and fellowship to develop a better understanding within the youth.

OBJECTIVE GORURBAN

In India, there is a huge divide between the rural and the urban worlds. This is marked by a strong difference not only in the environment and people’s livelihoods but also in language and culture. On one hand, rural communities can be very isolated. People often belong to scheduled tribes, which have historically been marginalized. However, they are sometimes not even aware of their marginalization. On the other hand, urban youth are typically not aware of what living outside the city means, where the food they eat is coming from, and what it is like to live in a rural community. The concept of Go Rurban took shape in 2017 during a Yatra in the Chambal region with the founder of Ekta Parishad and Activist for Peace Rajagopal PV. Here villages are populated by Sehriya Tribe in Sheopur and Shivpuri in Madhya Pradesh. Urban youth participating in the Yatra came up with the idea to organize camps where urban youth could explore and experience rural life, better understanding rural people’s struggles for land and other livelihoods. This aimed to develop awareness among the urban youth of the deeper reasons for the ongoing non-violent protests for land rights, peace, and justice.
